first timers

Your first class, demystified.

Reformer pilates looks intimidating from the outside. It isn't. Here's exactly how the first 60 minutes will go — so you can spend less time worrying and more time moving.

step one

Start with foundations, not the signature flow.

We strongly recommend brand-new movers start with foundations · new client — a slower, capped, instructor-led intro to the reformer. You'll learn the carriage, the springs, the four base positions, and the safety basics so your second class isn't a guessing game.

Already practiced reformer elsewhere? You can dive straight into a flow class — just tell us at check-in.

prep · the night before

Six small things to get right.

None of this is rocket science. But getting the small things right means you'll walk in calm — and walk out hooked.

  1. 1
    Arrive 10 minutes early.

    Check-in is fast, but we want you on a reformer with springs already set when class starts. The door locks at the top of the hour — quietly, but firmly.

  2. 2
    Wear something form-fitting.

    Loose tops fall over your face in inversions. Leggings or fitted shorts and a snug top are perfect. No zippers on the back of your pants — they scratch the carriage.

  3. 3
    Grip socks are required.

    Forgot yours? We stock a beautiful pair in the studio for $18. After class one, you'll own three pairs anyway.

  4. 4
    Bring water. Skip the heavy breakfast.

    Hydrate the morning of, eat something light an hour before, and you'll feel strong start to finish. We have filtered water on-site.

  5. 5
    Tell us about injuries.

    Recent surgery, pregnancy, disc issues, wrist or knee pain — quietly let your instructor know at check-in. We'll modify on the fly without making it a thing.

  6. 6
    Phones stay in the locker.

    The room is small, the class is intimate, and the music is the soundtrack. Trust us — you'll feel the difference.

what to expect

A 50-minute reformer class, narrated.

The pace is steady. The cues are clear. The hardest part is usually the warm-up — the rest builds on top of it.

0–5 min · settle

Breath work, spine articulation, and a slow warm-up on the carriage. You'll feel your obliques light up before you've done a single "exercise."

5–20 min · lower body

Footwork, glutes, hamstrings, and a calf series you'll feel for two days. The reformer makes these targeted in a way the floor can't.

20–35 min · core & arms

Long-lever planks, pull-throughs, and arm work using the cables. Quiet, slow, harder than it looks.

35–45 min · spice

A short, sharp block — usually glutes, obliques, or a balance challenge. This is where you'll catch yourself smiling mid-burn.

45–50 min · close

A long, slow stretch and a final breath. You'll wipe down your reformer, change your springs back, and float out the door.

After class

Your instructor will check in, answer questions, and (if you ask) tell you exactly which class to book next.

questions, answered

The things you'd text a friend who's already done it.

Can't find your question? Email hello@myflowhouse.com — we read everything.

I've never done pilates. Is this for me?

Yes — and especially yes. Reformer pilates is one of the most beginner-friendly strength practices on the planet because the machine supports you while it challenges you. Start with foundations and you'll be running by class three.

Is reformer harder than mat pilates?

It's different. The reformer gives you resistance and feedback that mat work can't, so the same exercise can feel both easier (because of the support) and harder (because the resistance is tunable). Most people find it more rewarding because they feel it working.

What's the difference between flow and barre?

flow uses the reformer for full-body strength, mobility, and control. house · barre uses the ballet barre and small props for low-impact, high-intensity isometric work — long-line legs, glutes, and core. Most members rotate between both.

How fit do I need to be to start?

Not fit at all. We program for every body — including people coming back from injury, post-partum, or who haven't worked out in years. Just tell your instructor at check-in.

Is the studio women-only?

No. flowhouse is a women-led studio with a beautifully diverse community — we welcome everyone who wants to move with intention.

What's the cancellation policy?

How do I book?

Do you offer private sessions?

Yes. One-on-one and small-group private reformer sessions are available by request — email us for current rates and availability. Great for prep before a wedding, post-partum return, or a specific goal.
