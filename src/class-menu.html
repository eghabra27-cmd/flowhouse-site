Intro teaches you how to use the FlowFormer and introduces the primary positions and movements used throughout class. Your instructor explains proper setup, alignment, transitions, breathing, spring changes, and the general sequence of a flowhouse class.

You'll practice movements from each major section of class and finish with a short five-to-ten-minute guided Flow, so you leave prepared for Foundation or Signature.

Signature combines contemporary Pilates with modern strength training through slow tempo, continuous transitions, and real time under tension. It's challenging, athletic, and intentional from first spring to last.

good to know

A little flowhouse language.

A few terms you'll hear in the studio — so your first class feels familiar before you arrive.

Flow FormerOur custom reformer.

The matte-black machine at the centre of every class, built specifically for the way we teach — carriage, platform, springs, and straps working together.

Time under tensionSlow, controlled reps.

Moving slowly and with control so muscles work for longer through each rep. It's how we build strength without impact.

The FlowThe rhythm of class.

A continuous, breath-led sequence that carries you from one movement to the next — the signature rhythm of a flowhouse class.

Springs & resistanceYour load, guided.

The reformer's springs set your resistance. Your instructor guides every change, so you're always working at the right load.

ModificationMeeting you where you are.

A small adjustment to a movement — a different range, position, or spring — so every body can work safely and effectively.

ProgressionThe next step up.

A way to add challenge to a movement once form is solid — more range, more load, or a longer hold, always earned.

Eccentric movementThe lengthening phase.

The lengthening phase of a movement. We slow it down on purpose — long eccentrics are where lasting strength is built.
