the flowhouse method

Where pilates meets strength.

We take the precision, breath, and control of classical reformer work and program it with the intent of modern strength training — slow, deliberate tempo and real time under tension that builds lasting strength.

Nothing is rushed and nothing is random. Every class is one continuous, breath-led flow — low impact by design, athletic by intention. You'll leave stronger, taller, and calmer than you walked in.

More on the method & the FlowFormer
choose your class

One method, many classes.

Every class shares the same intention — the difference is tempo, focus, and length. New to the reformer or years in, there's a starting point here for you.

Intro

First class

Learn the FlowFormer and the foundations of a flowhouse class from the ground up.

Best for
First-timers
Length
50 min
Focus
Setup & form
Level
New

Intro teaches you how to use the FlowFormer and introduces the primary positions and movements used throughout class. Your instructor explains proper setup, alignment, transitions, breathing, spring changes, and the general sequence of a flowhouse class.

You'll practice movements from each major section of class and finish with a short five-to-ten-minute guided Flow, so you leave prepared for Foundation or Signature.

Foundation

Beginner

A slower-paced, more instructional version of the method — with a full-body workout.

Best for

50 min

Foundation provides detailed cueing, additional setup support, and the time to understand each movement — while still delivering a complete, full-body workout. The pace is a touch slower so the fundamentals have room to land.

It's the natural next step after Intro, and the class that prepares you to move into Signature with confidence.

Signature combines contemporary Pilates with modern strength training through slow tempo, continuous transitions, and real time under tension. It's challenging, athletic, and intentional from first spring to last.

before your first class

A few things to know.

Everything you need for a calm, confident first visit. Your instructor guides the rest.

Grip socks

Required for safety and hygiene. Available for purchase in the studio if you need a pair.

Arrive early

First-timers should arrive about ten minutes before class for a short orientation and FlowFormer setup.

What to bring

Bring water and wear comfortable, fitted movement clothing you can move freely in.

Spring changes

Your instructor guides every spring adjustment. There's no need to change resistance until directed.

Modifications

Modifications are always available. Let your instructor know about any injuries or concerns before class.

Phones

Please keep phones silent and stored away so the room stays calm and focused for everyone.

The FlowFormer

Our custom reformer. Your instructor will walk you through the carriage, platform, springs, straps, and safety features.

Class flow

Classes follow an intentional sequence. Your instructor guides every movement, transition, and spring change from start to finish.

good to know

A little flowhouse language.

A few terms you'll hear in the studio — so your first class feels familiar before you arrive.

Flow FormerOur custom reformer.

The matte-black machine at the centre of every class, built specifically for the way we teach — carriage, platform, springs, and straps working together.

Time under tensionSlow, controlled reps.

Moving slowly and with control so muscles work for longer through each rep. It's how we build strength without impact.

The FlowThe rhythm of class.

A continuous, breath-led sequence that carries you from one movement to the next — the signature rhythm of a flowhouse class.

Springs & resistanceYour load, guided.

The reformer's springs set your resistance. Your instructor guides every change, so you're always working at the right load.

ModificationMeeting you where you are.

A small adjustment to a movement — a different range, position, or spring — so every body can work safely and effectively.

ProgressionThe next step up.

A way to add challenge to a movement once form is solid — more range, more load, or a longer hold, always earned.

Eccentric movementThe lengthening phase.

The lengthening phase of a movement. We slow it down on purpose — long eccentrics are where lasting strength is built.
