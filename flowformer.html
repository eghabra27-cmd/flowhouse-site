the flowformer · our method

The reformer, sequenced like a sentence.

Most studios use the reformer as a piece of equipment. We use it as a language — and we built the Flowformer method to speak it fluently. Every class is structured around one muscle conversation, one breath rhythm, one quietly transformative hour.

Signature method
three principles

Built on pilates. Programmed for the way you actually live.

Joseph Pilates was a movement obsessive. So are we. The Flowformer takes his original principles — control, concentration, precision, breath, flow, centering — and re-sequences them for thirty-something coastal lives.

01 · sequence

One muscle conversation per class.

Each Flowformer class is built around a single anatomical theme — glutes, obliques, posterior chain, deep core. You'll feel the next morning what you worked the night before. No "everything everywhere all at once" sprawl.

02 · spring

Resistance, never momentum.

We program four spring tensions per class — heavy, medium-heavy, medium, light — so every exercise lands in the muscle, not in joint shock. The reformer rewards control. We reward control.

03 · pace

Slow on purpose.

A six-second eccentric beats a thirty-rep blitz. Our pacing trains deep stabilizing muscles you didn't know you owned. That's why the practice scales — it gets harder as you get stronger.

a flowformer class

Five blocks. Fifty minutes. One conversation.

The shape of every Flowformer class. You'll know within three classes which block is your favorite — and which one your body needs the most.

  1. 1
    Settle · 5 min.

    Breath, spine articulation, gentle reformer warmup. We meet you where your body is today, not where it was yesterday.

  2. 2
    Lower body · 15 min.

    Footwork series, glutes, hamstrings, calf work. The reformer is unmatched for targeted lower-body work — and it's the safest place on earth to load a sore knee.

  3. 3
    Core & arms · 15 min.

    Long-lever planks, pull-throughs, cable arm work. Quiet, slow, harder than it looks — and the reason you'll see definition you've never seen before.

  4. 4
    Spice · 10 min.

    A short, sharp finisher tied to the day's muscle theme. Sometimes it's glute pulses. Sometimes it's an oblique series that makes you laugh out loud.

  5. 5
    Close · 5 min.

    A long, slow stretch and a final breath. You'll wipe down your reformer, change springs back, and float out the door taller than you walked in.

why it works

The Flowformer is the workout you can do forever.

Most fitness wears the body down. The Flowformer builds it up. Reformer pilates is one of the only practices that gets harder as you get stronger, easier as you get older, and more rewarding as you get more attuned to your own body.
