the class flow

Six blocks. One conversation with the body.

Every Signature and Foundation class is structured this way — sequenced for time under tension, continuous movement, and intentional return.

  1. 1
    Center · core activation

    Breath work and deep-core engagement on the Flowformer. We start the conversation from the inside out — finding the muscles that hold every other movement together.

  2. 2
    Lower body block

    Footwork, glutes, hamstrings. Time-under-tension reps on the carriage — slow eccentrics, intentional control, low-impact strength.

  3. 3
    Obliques

    Side-lying work, rotational planks, kneeling oblique series. The most quietly powerful block — and the one you'll feel two days later.

  4. 4
    Upper body block

    Long-lever arms on the cables, push-pull symmetry, breath-led pacing. Strength without strain.

  5. 5
    Final core push · center abdominals

    One last deliberate burn. Centered, low-impact, high-intensity — the moment you'll catch yourself smiling mid-flow.

  6. 6
    Slow stretch + cool down

    Long-line opening, breath, nervous-system regulation. You'll wipe the carriage, reset, and walk out taller than you came in.

a note on barre

Barre is beginner-friendly, low impact, posture-enhancing, strength-focused — and a perfect complement to your reformer practice.

Two barre walls, twenty-five spots per Barre 45 class, continuous movement, and intentional burn. It belongs alongside the reformer, not behind it.

questions, answered

The things you'd text a friend who's already done it.

Can't find your question? Email info@flowhouserb.com — we read everything.

What's the difference between Signature and Barre 45?

Signature is our contemporary reformer pilates class on the Flowformer — full-body, time-under-tension, sequenced. Barre 45 is a strength-based barre class on the wall using small props and continuous movement. Different equipment, same flowhouse philosophy: precision-based, low impact, high intensity.

What's the difference between Foundation and Signature?

Foundation is the slower, more cued version of Signature — built for new reformer movers and for refining the basics. You'll learn the Flowformer setup, the springs, and our foundational positions. Signature assumes you know the carriage and moves at a steadier, more continuous pace.

What is Yin?

Yin is our restorative reformer-based stretch and recovery class, inspired by yin yoga principles. Slow holds, long lines, breath-led pacing, nervous-system regulation. The class your body books for itself after a hard week.

What should beginners start with?

Start with Foundation if you're new to reformer pilates, or grab a seat in our occasional Intro class. Both are paced and cued for first-timers. Once you've taken either one, Signature, Yin, and Barre 45 are all open to you.

I've never done pilates. Is this for me?

Yes — and especially yes. Reformer pilates is one of the most beginner-friendly strength practices because the machine supports you while it challenges you.

How fit do I need to be to start?

Not fit at all. We program for every body — including people coming back from injury, post-partum, or who haven't moved in years. Just tell your instructor at check-in.

Is the studio women-only?

No. flowhouse is a women-led studio with a beautifully diverse community. We welcome everyone who wants to move with intention.
