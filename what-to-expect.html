what to expect

Your first class, demystified.

The Flowformer looks intimidating from the outside. It isn't. Here is exactly how the first hour goes — and what happens after.

step one

Start with Foundation, not Signature.

We recommend brand-new movers start with Foundation — a slower, capped, instructor-led intro on the Flowformer. You'll learn the carriage, the springs, and our foundational positions so your second class isn't a guessing game.

Already practiced reformer elsewhere? You can step straight into Signature — just tell us at check-in.

Book Foundation Or grab the 2-week intro
the class flow

Six blocks. One conversation with the body.

Every Signature and Foundation class is structured this way. Time under tension, continuous movement, intentional sequencing — so what you feel in class is what you feel the next morning.

  1. 1
    Center · core activation

    Breath work and deep-core engagement on the Flowformer. We start the conversation from the inside out — finding the muscles that hold every other movement together.

  2. 2
    Lower body block

    Footwork, glutes, hamstrings. Time-under-tension reps on the carriage — slow eccentrics, intentional control, the reformer's strongest argument.

  3. 3
    Obliques

    Side-lying work, rotational planks, kneeling oblique series. The most quietly powerful block — and the one you'll feel two days later.

  4. 4
    Upper body block

    Long-lever arms on the cables, push-pull symmetry, breath-led pacing. Strength without strain.

  5. 5
    Final core push · center abdominals

    One last deliberate burn. Centered, low-impact, high-intensity — the moment you'll catch yourself smiling mid-flow.

  6. 6
    Slow stretch + cool down

    Long-line opening, breath, nervous-system regulation. You'll wipe the carriage, reset the springs, and walk out taller than you came in.

after class

More than a workout. A social and wellness ritual.

flowhouse is a sanctuary, but also a community. Stay after class. Grab a juice. Chat with the founding circle. Linger in the calm. The hour on the Flowformer is the start of the ritual — not the end of it.

We protect the calm inside the studio and we welcome you into the community right outside it. Both are part of the experience.

prep · the night before

Five small things to get right.

Get the small things right and you'll walk in calm — and walk out hooked.

  1. 1
    Arrive 10 minutes early.

    We want you on the Flowformer with springs set before class starts. The door locks at the top of the hour — quietly, but firmly.

  2. 2
    Wear something form-fitting.

    Leggings or fitted shorts and a snug top. No zippers on the back of pants — they catch the carriage. The flowhouse line will be available in studio.

  3. 3
    Grip socks are required.

    We stock a beautiful pair in the studio for $18. After class one, you'll own three pairs anyway.

  4. 4
    Bring water. Skip the heavy breakfast.

    Hydrate the morning of, eat something light an hour before. We have filtered water on-site.

  5. 5
    Tell us about injuries.

    Recent surgery, pregnancy, disc issues, joint pain — quietly let your instructor know at check-in. We'll modify without making it a thing.

questions, answered

The things you'd text a friend who's already done it.

Can't find your question? Email info@flowhouserb.com — we read everything.

What's the difference between Signature and Barre?

Signature is our contemporary reformer pilates class on the Flowformer — full-body, time-under-tension, sequenced. Barre 45 is a strength-based barre class on the wall using small props and continuous movement. Different equipment, same flowhouse philosophy: precision-based, low impact, high intensity.

What's the difference between Foundation and Signature?

Foundation is the slower, more cued version of Signature — built for new reformer movers. You'll learn the Flowformer setup, the springs, and our foundational positions. Signature assumes you know the carriage and moves at a steadier, more continuous pace.

What is Yin?

Yin is our restorative reformer-based stretch and recovery class, inspired by yin yoga principles. Slow holds, long lines, breath-led pacing, nervous-system regulation. The class your body books for itself after a hard week.

What should beginners start with?

Start with Foundation if you're new to reformer pilates, or grab a seat in our occasional Intro class. Both are paced and cued for first-timers. Once you've taken either one, Signature, Yin, and Barre 45 are all open to you.

I've never done pilates. Is this for me?

Yes — and especially yes. Reformer pilates is one of the most beginner-friendly strength practices because the machine supports you while it challenges you. Start with Foundation and you'll be in Signature by class three.

How fit do I need to be to start?

Not fit at all. We program for every body — including people coming back from injury, post-partum, or who haven't moved in years. Just tell your instructor at check-in.

Is the studio women-only?

No. flowhouse is a women-led studio with a beautifully diverse community. We welcome everyone who wants to move with intention.

What's the cancellation policy?

Free cancellation up to 12 hours before class. Late cancels (inside 12 hours) cost $15. No-shows are $20. Unlimited members get a small monthly grace allowance — details on the pricing page.

How do I book?

Through our app at launch, or right here on the schedule. Founding members get a 14-day booking window; everyone else gets 7 days.
